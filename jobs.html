Astrophysicists study the physical properties and behavior of celestial objects and phenomena. They use theoretical models and observational data to understand the universe.

Planetary scientists study planets, moons, and other bodies in our solar system and beyond. They investigate their composition, geology, and potential for life.

Satellite systems engineers design and manage satellite systems and their components. They ensure that satellites operate correctly and meet mission objectives.
5 / 10
Space mission planners develop plans and strategies for space missions, including spacecraft trajectories, mission timelines, and resource allocation.
6 / 10
Rocket propulsion engineers design and test propulsion systems for rockets and spacecraft. They focus on optimizing performance and ensuring reliability.
7 / 10
Space robotics engineers develop robotic systems used in space exploration, such as rovers and robotic arms. They work on automation, control systems, and integration with spacecraft.
8 / 10
Space scientists conduct research on various aspects of space, including cosmic radiation, solar phenomena, and the interstellar medium. They often work with space missions and observatories.
9 / 10
Astronauts are trained to travel and work in space. They conduct scientific experiments, perform spacewalks, and operate spacecraft.
10 / 10
Space operations specialists manage the day-to-day operations of spacecraft and space missions. They handle communication with spacecraft, monitor systems, and coordinate with ground teams.
