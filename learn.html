Exploring Spacecraft Safety

Building a Safer Future: Key Readings to Understand Space Missions

1.1 Life Support System

Function: Maintains oxygen levels, pressure, and removes carbon dioxide. Importance: Critical for the survival of astronauts in space.

1.2 Propulsion System

Function: Provides thrust to maneuver the spacecraft. Importance: Essential for trajectory adjustments and orbital changes.

1.3 Navigation System

Function: Calculates and adjusts spacecraft position and trajectory. Importance: Ensures the spacecraft follows its intended path and reaches its destination.

1.4 Communication System

Function: Maintains contact between the spacecraft and ground control. Importance: Vital for transmitting data, receiving commands, and ensuring mission coordination.

1.5 Power Supply System

Function: Generates and distributes electrical power to all spacecraft systems. Importance: Powers all operational systems and instruments.

1.6 Thermal Control System

Function: Regulates the spacecraft's temperature by managing heat dissipation and insulation. Importance: Prevents overheating or freezing of spacecraft components.

1.7 Science Instruments

Function: Conducts experiments and collects data about space environments and celestial bodies. Importance: Provides valuable scientific information and insights.

1.8 Structural Framework

Function: Supports and protects the spacecraft's components and crew. Importance: Ensures the spacecraft's integrity and safety during the mission.

2.1 Radiation Exposure

Risk: Spacecraft and astronauts are exposed to high levels of cosmic radiation. Safety Measure: Use radiation shielding materials and limit exposure time.

2.2 Micrometeoroid and Orbital Debris (MMOD)

Risk: Spacecraft can be hit by micrometeoroids or debris, causing damage. Safety Measure: Implement shielding and track debris to avoid collisions.

2.3 Equipment Failure

Risk: Systems and components may fail, impacting spacecraft operations. Safety Measure: Design redundant systems and perform regular maintenance checks.

2.4 Thermal Extremes

Risk: Spacecraft can experience extreme temperatures in space. Safety Measure: Use thermal control systems to regulate temperatures.

2.5 Loss of Communication

Risk: Communication with ground control may be lost. Safety Measure: Use multiple communication channels and backup systems.

2.7 Fire Hazard

Risk: Fire in the confined environment of a spacecraft is a severe risk. Safety Measure: Use fire detection and suppression systems, and have emergency protocols in place.
